SSL Certificate FAQs

Get answers to common questions about SSL certificates

An SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) certificate is a digital certificate that authenticates a website's identity and enables an encrypted connection. It creates a secure link between a web server and a browser, ensuring that all data passed between them remains private and integral.

You need an SSL certificate to:

  • Protect sensitive customer information
  • Establish trust with your visitors
  • Improve your search engine rankings (Google favors HTTPS sites)
  • Meet PCI compliance requirements if you accept payments
  • Prevent browser warnings about insecure connections

The main differences are in the validation process and level of trust displayed:

  • Domain Validation (DV): Basic validation that verifies you own the domain. Issued quickly (minutes). Shows padlock icon.
  • Organization Validation (OV): Verifies your business identity. Takes 1-3 days. Shows organization name in certificate details.
  • Extended Validation (EV): Rigorous verification process. Takes 5-7 days. Displays your company name in the browser address bar (green bar in some browsers).

We make SSL installation easy:

  1. Purchase your SSL certificate
  2. Generate a Certificate Signing Request (CSR) from your hosting control panel
  3. Complete the validation process (varies by certificate type)
  4. We'll issue your certificate
  5. Install the certificate via your hosting control panel (we provide instructions)
  6. Update your site to use HTTPS
