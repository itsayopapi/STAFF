Objection Handling

Advanced techniques to overcome even the toughest objections

"Your prices are too high"

Response Strategy:

  1. Acknowledge: "I understand budget is important..."
  2. Reframe: "When you look at total cost of ownership..."
  3. Compare: "Compared to managing this yourself..."
  4. Justify: "The premium comes from these value-adds..."
  5. Option: "We have budget-friendly options..."

Verbatim Examples:

  • "Our customers find they actually save money through..."
  • "Let me show our ROI calculator..."
  • "Would operational expense vs capital expense help?"
"Migrating is too complicated"

Response Strategy:

  1. Empathize: "I understand change can be daunting..."
  2. Assure: "We've migrated 5,000+ sites..."
  3. Detail: "Our process includes..."
  4. Guarantee: "We guarantee zero downtime..."
  5. Support: "Our team handles everything..."

Verbatim Examples:

  • "We completely handle migrations for free..."
  • "Here's our step-by-step migration checklist..."
  • "We'll schedule at your convenience..."
"You're missing [specific feature]"

Response Strategy:

  1. Acknowledge: "That's valuable feedback..."
  2. Redirect: "Our platform provides [alternative]..."
  3. Explore: "How are you using that feature?"
  4. Roadmap: "We're adding features like..."
  5. Custom: "We can customize a solution..."

Verbatim Examples:

  • "While we don't have that exact feature..."
  • "Many customers use [alternative] instead..."
  • "That's on our roadmap for Q3..."
"We're happy with our current provider"

Response Strategy:

  1. Validate: "That's great to hear..."
  2. Differentiate: "Many customers switched for..."
  3. Compare: "Would you be open to a comparison?"
  4. Probe: "What do you like about them?"
  5. Offer: "We can do a free trial..."

Verbatim Examples:

  • "What would make you consider switching?"
  • "Here's how we compare on key metrics..."
  • "Many clients kept their old provider as backup..."

Objection Handling Framework

  1. Listen: Let them fully express the objection
  2. Acknowledge: Validate their concern
  3. Clarify: Ask probing questions
  4. Respond: Address with facts/alternatives
  5. Confirm: Check if resolved

Prevention Strategies
