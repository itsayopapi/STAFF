Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our SEO analyzer

Our SEO analyzer uses industry-standard metrics and data from multiple sources to provide highly accurate analysis. We check 50+ SEO factors including technical elements, content quality, backlinks, and user experience metrics.

While no tool can guarantee 100% accuracy (as search algorithms constantly change), our analysis correlates strongly with actual search performance based on feedback from thousands of users.

We recommend running a full SEO analysis at least once a month to monitor your website's health and identify new opportunities. However, the ideal frequency depends on your website:

  • New websites: Weekly for the first 3 months
  • Active blogs/content sites: Every 2 weeks
  • Static business websites: Monthly
  • After major updates: Immediately after significant changes

Yes, we take your privacy and data security very seriously:

  • We don't store your website content or sensitive data
  • All analyses are encrypted in transit
  • We don't share your data with third parties
  • You can request deletion of your analysis history at any time

For paid plans, we use industry-standard security measures to protect your payment information and account details.

While Google Search Console provides valuable data straight from Google, our SEO analyzer offers several advantages:

  • More comprehensive: We check 50+ factors beyond what GSC reports
  • Actionable recommendations: We don't just show problems, we explain how to fix them
  • Competitor analysis: Compare your performance against competitors
  • Historical tracking: Paid plans include trend analysis over time
  • Simplified reporting: We consolidate complex data into easy-to-understand insights

For best results, we recommend using both tools together.

Our analyzer can only access publicly available websites that are reachable from the open internet. This means:

  • Password-protected sites: Cannot be analyzed unless you provide access credentials (available in paid plans)
  • Local development sites: Must be publicly accessible to be analyzed
  • Intranet sites: Cannot be analyzed as they're not publicly accessible
  • Staging environments: Can be analyzed if made publicly available

For local development, we recommend using tools like Lighthouse directly in your browser.
