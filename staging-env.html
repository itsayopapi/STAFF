Frequently Asked Questions

How many staging environments can I create?

Our starter plan includes 3 active staging environments, while professional and enterprise plans offer 10 and unlimited environments respectively. Archived environments don't count toward your limit.

What's the difference between a staging environment and a development environment?

A staging environment is an exact copy of your production environment used for final testing before changes go live. A development environment is typically used earlier in the process for building and initial testing of new features, and may have different configurations.

Can I test email functionality in my staging environment?

Yes! All staging environments include a mail catcher that captures outgoing emails without actually sending them. You can review all captured emails in the environment dashboard, and even resend them to your team for testing.

How do I prevent search engines from indexing my staging site?

All staging environments automatically include noindex, nofollow meta tags and are blocked from search engine crawlers by default. Additionally, we recommend using password protection or IP restrictions for sensitive projects.

What happens to my staging environment when I deploy to production?

By default, staging environments remain active after deployment so you can continue testing. You can manually archive environments when they're no longer needed, or set up auto-archiving rules based on inactivity periods.

Can I clone a staging environment to create a new one?

Absolutely! You can create new environments from production, from other staging environments, or from snapshots. This is useful for creating parallel testing environments or preserving specific states.
